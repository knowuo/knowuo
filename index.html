Health

2 resources

University Health Services

1590 E 13th Ave

Did you know that you can meet many of your health and mental health needs without leaving campus? UHS offers medical care, counseling services, well-being programming, and much more. They are a nationally accredited, multi-specialty healthcare organization with over 150 staff whose primary focus is the health and well-being of UO students.

University Counseling Services

541-346-32271590 E 13th Ave

Call for 24-hour crisis support. Sign up for one of thirteen group therapy sections, short-term one-on-one sessions with a therapist, or work with a case manager to find community therapists or clinics for long-term support.

Food

6 resources

Ducks Feeding Ducks

An emergency food resource — apply up to twice per term to receive 12 Duck Bucks for food. Additional help may be offered through the Office of the Dean of Students on a case-by-case basis.

Visit site

Food For Lane County

If you are a single-person household with an annual income below $47,880, you qualify to receive food from partner pantries or mobile pantry. Free meals at meal sites are available with no eligibility required. Use their food finder to locate pantries and meal sites near you.

Visit site

Produce Drop

EMU AmphitheatreTuesdays @ 3pm

Stop by to grab free groceries and supplies! Find fruits and vegetables, baked goods, and even toilet paper. Nearly all undergraduate students qualify for this resource.

Visit site

Hearth and Table

1857 Potter St541-345-0395Thursdays 6:30–7:30pm

Come to Central Lutheran Church every Thursday of the academic term for a fresh-cooked meal with community members (6:30–7:30pm) or choose meal pick-up (6:45–7:30pm) if you prefer not to dine in.

Visit site

Grocery Shuttle

Sign up through Calendly for a free shuttle ride for UO students to affordable grocery stores each odd week of the term. Make sure to bring a reusable shopping bag!

Sign up

Leftover Textover

Sign up to get texts about leftovers from UO catering events. Texts are sent approximately 15 minutes in advance with a campus location to pick up food from.

UO Police Department Safety Escorts

541-346-291924/7

UO Police Department staff can provide safety escorts to students and employees from any campus location to any other campus location, 24 hours per day. Escorts may be performed by student security assistants, community service officers, or police officers.
